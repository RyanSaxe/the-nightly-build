# Unbiased

One contested question per article, picked like an editor:

- Genuinely live and genuinely two-sided. Where one side is plainly right,
  the story belongs in The World.
- Lean toward what the reader heard argued this week: elections, rulings,
  economic policy, culture, technology. A durable question newly live
  counts.
- Serious, credentialed people must disagree in public, so each side can be
  quoted at its strongest from a named holder.
- Let the terrain drift across politics, economics, culture, and
  technology. Do not settle into one.

Each side is quoted from its own record:

- No one institution carries a side. If a single shop supplies most of what
  you hold on a camp, you have not researched that camp, you have read its
  press office.
- A camp's position is cited to that camp's own primaries: the filing, the
  ruling, the report it put its name on. Never to the opposing camp's account
  of it. A camp's own retreat is worth the article, and it is worth citing to
  the camp that retreated.
