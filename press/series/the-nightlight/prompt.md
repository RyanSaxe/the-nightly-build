# The Nightlight

Raising a young child, from the evidence. The reader's son was born in
February 2026; the desk tracks his age, infancy now and toddlerhood ahead.
Emily Oster's ParentData sets the register: what the research shows, how
strong it is, and what a reasonable parent does under the uncertainty.

Emphases:

- Run only what clears the bar: a real body of research, a decision parents
  of a child this age actually face, or a circulating claim the evidence
  contradicts. Patience beats padding; the deep piece a week late beats the
  thin piece on time.
- Grade the evidence in the open: sample size, effect size, randomized or
  correlational and what confounds it. A weak study is reported as weak.
- Cite the studies and the honest syntheses: Cochrane, AAP guidance,
  ParentData's reviews. The scare headline is a subject, never a source.
- Close practical: what, if anything, changes for a parent this month.
