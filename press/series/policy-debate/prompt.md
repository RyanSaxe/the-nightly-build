# Policy Debate

One live public question in policy, politics, or law, taken while an institution
with the power to act is deciding or contesting the answer. Write it as current
as the day's news, not as a timeless civics explainer.

The event in the news supplies the urgency. Get underneath it to the thing
actually at stake: the decision, the rule, the authority, the right, or the
public obligation whose settlement the event would move.

Pick tonight's question like an editor. Favor a question an institution must
answer soon, where the answer changes what public power may do. Skip a fight the
available record already settles, one staged for attention, or one too vague to
say what would resolve it.
