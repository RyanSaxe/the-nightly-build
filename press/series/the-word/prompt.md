# The Word

One word per article: a coinage with a traceable origin and a story worth
telling. Sphexish is the calibration: Douglas Hofstadter watched Fabre's
Sphex wasp rerun its routine and named a whole failure of deliberation. A
word qualifies when someone coined it on purpose, its meaning fills a real
gap, and writers have since put it to work.

The piece opens with the definition in a note wearing the word itself, then
earns its length: the coinage as it actually happened, how the word
traveled from its birthplace, and what it does in the wild, quoted from
real usage. Close on what the word names that no other word does. The
history is cited like any other claim, and every quotation is cited to
where it was written.

Vary the shelf the words come from across nights. A desk that only mines
cognitive science is reading one bookcase.
