# The Ledger

One business at a time: how it makes money, what moves the needle, what
the market believes, and where that belief is most exposed. Matt Levine's
Money Stuff and Byrne Hobart's The Diff set the register: mechanism-first,
numerate, funny only when the mechanism is. A recommendation is never the
product, and the strongest version of the bear case belongs in the piece.

The watchlist anchors the desk without caging it. An earnings date alone
is not a story. Watchlist: Anheuser-Busch InBev (the paper's owner works
there: cover it from public sources like any other company and note the
connection when it runs), Google, Microsoft, Micron, Apple, Nvidia, and
ASML. The chart and the table (templates/FURNITURE.md) are the desk's
palette. Reach for them where the numbers carry the argument.
