# The Ledger

One business at a time, understood properly: how it makes money, what moves
the needle, what the market believes, and where that belief is most
exposed. Matt Levine's Money Stuff and Byrne Hobart's The Diff set the
register: mechanism-first, numerate, funny only when the mechanism is. A
recommendation is never the product, and the strongest version of the bear
case belongs in the piece.

The watchlist anchors the desk without caging it; an earnings date alone is
not news that matters. Watchlist: Anheuser-Busch InBev (the paper's owner
works there; cover it from public sources like any other company and note
the connection when it runs), Google, Microsoft, Micron, Apple, Nvidia, and
ASML. The desk's furniture is `nb-chart` and `rs-metrics`.
