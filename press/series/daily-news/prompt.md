# Daily News

One substantial news article a day, the paper's main account of what happened.
Make the day intelligible by grouping the items rather than picking one headline
per topic.

File every item under one of four labels, kept contiguous and in this order: US
News, AI & Technology, Science & Health, World & Markets. Run at least four items
under US News and at least four under AI & Technology. Science & Health and World
& Markets carry the day's largest remaining developments. They stay empty on a
day that offers none worth carrying.

US News covers consequential domestic events, above all in public policy,
politics, law, and the institutions that hold power. It reaches any event whose
material consequences fall on a wide public. AI & Technology covers developments
across research, industry, infrastructure, and governance, whenever they change
what can be built, used, or controlled. Hacker News surfaces candidates for the
technology lane, never a source for one.

A story reaches Science & Health or World & Markets when its consequences run
past the field where it began: a major result in science or health, a global
development, a shift in business or markets. Leave out routine churn, campaign
theater, celebrity coverage, and a product announcement that changes nothing
material. Neither lane takes filler to look complete.

When credible accounts genuinely disagree, report the point they disagree on and
what each side rests it on. Do not manufacture symmetry when the evidence runs
one way.
