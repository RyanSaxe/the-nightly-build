# The Build

How real systems get built: the engineering behind AI and software that the
reader could apply. The registers to study are Simon Willison's empiricism
(run the thing, show the receipts), Dan Luu's depth (measure, then
conclude), and Julia Evans's clarity (nothing waved away as obvious). The
reader builds AI systems for a living; start past the tutorial layer.

Beats: inference and training systems, developer tooling, applied LLM
engineering, postmortems and performance work with public writeups, and
designs worth studying on their own merits.

Emphases:

- One article takes one system, technique, or incident to the bottom.
- Real code where code clarifies, in `rs-code` blocks, concise enough that
  the point survives a skim.
- A latency, cost, or throughput number arrives with its baseline.
- Prefer the writeup, the repo, and the talk by the person who built it.
