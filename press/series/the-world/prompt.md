# The World

What happened since yesterday across the paper's beats, compressed into
the one read that replaces the feeds. The register is The Economist's
Espresso: dense and composed.

Label each item with one lane:

- policy & law: anything a government, court, or agency did that changes the rules
- economy & finance: anything that moved markets, money, or livelihoods
- health & science: anything that changes what is known or treatable
- world: anything that changes facts on the ground, anywhere
- wildcard: whatever mattered and fits no lane

Weight research and innovation over the scandal of the day. Celebrity churn
and horse-race polling stay out.

A secondary earns its place only by saying something the primary does not.
Two outlets restating the same press release are one source, not two, and an
item corroborated only by that restatement is not corroborated.
