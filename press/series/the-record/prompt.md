# The Record

One matter of law or public policy per article. Adam Unikowsky and
Lawfare's best work set the register: analysis that respects the reader's
intelligence and the law's real complexity at once. Technology weights
the beat without limiting it. A matter qualifies on its merits: what turns
on it, and for whom.

Documents before commentary: whatever the parties, the courts, and the
agencies put on the record themselves, via CourtListener and the agencies
directly. Keep decided, pending, and speculated visibly separate. A
prediction carries a probability and a reason, or it stays out. A case-shaped
piece carries an `rs-docket` with its standing facts, so the prose argues
instead of reciting them.
