Trump hits 60 economies with new tariffs over forced labor

Washington pressed on trade, Iran, and a Chinese AI lab at once, while oil topped $100 on the war and earnings forced a reckoning with the cost of AI.

5 min read2026-07-24
US News

Trump hits 60 economies with new tariffs over forced labor1

Tariffs of 10% to 12.5% took effect at 12:01 a.m. ET Friday on imports from about 60 trading partners, replacing the temporary worldwide 10% duty as it expired.2 The White House set them under Section 301 of the Trade Act of 1974, citing weak enforcement of forced-labor bans, and reached partners that together carry 99.4% of US trade.3 It is the widest trade action of Trump's second term, and it revives a trade regime the Supreme Court had dismantled in February.

US News

US strikes Iran a 13th straight night as Trump threatens more4

US Central Command completed its 13th consecutive night of strikes on Iranian military targets at 9 p.m. ET Thursday, a tally other outlets separately confirmed,5 hitting command centers, drone stores, communication networks, and coastal surveillance sites, and reported no casualties while saying the Strait of Hormuz "remains open for transit." Trump said he is weighing a "massive attack," claiming Tehran has not "received enough pain yet." Iran's foreign minister, Abbas Araghchi, rejected a US ceasefire relayed through Iraq and warned that "mindless aggression" would not aid a deal.6 Iranian media reported blasts in Khorramabad, Jask, Ahvaz, and Bandar Abbas, where India's Tribune put the toll at two injured, a figure CENTCOM does not give.7

US News

House adopts a $95 billion war-and-priorities budget, 216-2148

The House adopted a Republican budget blueprint 216-214 on Wednesday. The package clears up to $95 billion: about $73 billion for the Iran war, $12 billion in farm aid, and $10 billion to push states toward the SAVE America Act's voting rules.9 Republicans turned to reconciliation only after regular appropriations collapsed, and every Democrat opposed the measure, joined by Republicans Thomas Massie and Warren Davidson and independent Kevin Kiley.10 Outlets differ on how the total splits internally, so the 216-214 tally is the one firm number.

US News

Senate confirms a North Dakota judge to the Eighth Circuit, 48-4711

The Senate confirmed Daniel Mack Traynor of North Dakota to the Eighth Circuit Court of Appeals on Thursday, 48-47, with five senators not voting.12 The vote fell along party lines, the pattern the chamber now follows on appellate seats.13 The same day it confirmed Antonio Pozos to the federal district court in eastern Pennsylvania, 49-44, with Democrats Dick Durbin and John Fetterman among the yeas.

AI & Technology

Intel posts its fastest revenue growth in 15 years on AI chips14

Intel reported second-quarter revenue of $16.1 billion, up 25% from a year earlier and its fastest growth in more than 15 years, driven by data-center and AI revenue that rose 59% to $6.3 billion.15 Non-GAAP earnings were $0.42 a share against about $0.21 expected, third-quarter guidance of $15.8 to $16.8 billion topped consensus, and the stock jumped after hours.16 On a GAAP basis Intel still lost $2.16 a share, so the turnaround sits in the top line, not yet the bottom.

AI & Technology

Alphabet lifts AI spending toward $200 billion, and its stock falls17

Alphabet raised its 2026 capital-spending guidance to $195-205 billion, up from $180-190 billion, after second-quarter capex hit a record $44.9 billion and pushed free cash flow to negative $5.9 billion.18 Revenue rose 24% to $119.8 billion, beating estimates, with Google Cloud up 82%.19 The shares fell about 5% after hours: investors treated capital spending running near 42% of the annual revenue rate as the number that mattered, not the beat.

AI & Technology

OpenAI will build a $30 billion data center in Georgia20

OpenAI unveiled "Project Camellia," a data-center campus in Effingham County, Georgia, near Savannah, that it will design and build itself for the first time, backed by a Georgia Power contract for 3.2 gigawatts.21 The company put the initial cost near $20 billion and said the full build could top $30 billion, with power coming online from 2028 through 2032.22 It is still seeking financing and construction partners and has pledged local community funds, including $80 million for the county and Codex credits for Georgia students.

AI & Technology

US science chief accuses China's Moonshot of stealing Anthropic's model23

Michael Kratsios, the White House science director, publicly accused China's Moonshot AI of "large-scale, covert industrial distillation" of Anthropic's Fable model to build its Kimi K3, released July 17. He said Moonshot "likely relied on" Nvidia GB300 servers reached through Thailand in breach of US export controls.24 He offered no logs or procurement records, and some researchers note the roughly one-month gap between Fable and Kimi K3 is short for distillation at that scale. China's embassy called the allegations "entirely unfounded" and accused Washington of smearing China's AI progress.25 The charge builds on a February complaint from Anthropic and a Treasury warning of sanctions, but naming a specific company is new, and it rests on assertion rather than disclosed evidence.

Science & Health

FDA advisers back compounding popular peptides over the agency's objection26

An FDA advisory committee voted 8-6, with one abstention, to recommend adding the peptide BPC-157 to the list of substances that pharmacies may compound. It backed three more the same day, KPV, TB-500, and MOTS-c, over FDA staff who had urged rejecting all seven under review.27 The vote is non-binding and awaits the agency's decision.28 A recently reshaped panel with members tied to the peptide industry drew conflict-of-interest criticism, and the human-trial evidence for the compounds is thin, which puts the panel directly at odds with the agency's own scientists.

World & Markets

Oil tops $100 after Houthis attack two Saudi tankers29

Brent crude settled at $100.65 a barrel, up 6.96%, its first close above $100 since late May, after Yemen's Houthis struck two Saudi oil tankers, the Encelia and the Layla, in the Red Sea.30 The group's military spokesman said the missile-and-drone attack enforced a blockade and declared Bab el-Mandeb closed to Saudi-flagged tankers. Saudi Arabia's press agency confirmed only that the Encelia was hit and left ablaze, its crew safe, as about ten vessels changed course.31 A Windward analyst called the blockade selective, "shaping who moves Saudi crude, not whether it moves," and noted "you don't have to do a lot to get the oil markets to react."30

World & Markets

Tesla sets a delivery record as its margin collapses to 1.4%32

Tesla delivered a record 480,126 vehicles in the second quarter, up 25% and well above the roughly 406,600 expected, lifting revenue 26% to $28.24 billion.33 Profit went the other way: non-GAAP earnings of $0.33 a share missed the roughly $0.53 expected, operating income fell 57% to $398 million, and the operating margin sank to 1.4%.34 Free cash flow swung to negative $1.09 billion and the shares fell about 3% after hours, a market saying volume no longer pays for Tesla when each car earns almost nothing.
