The World

Strikes on the Strait, drones on the Azov

American bombs on Iran's Hormuz coast and Ukrainian drones on Russian tankers put a war premium back into crude, while Congo's Ebola dead pass 600 and OpenAI ships a work agent.

3 min read2026-07-11
World

US bombs Iran's Hormuz coast for a second straight night1

Central Command said it struck about 90 military targets across Iran overnight, after roughly 80 the night before, in response to Iran firing on three commercial tankers in the Strait of Hormuz. The UN says more than 6,000 sailors are trapped in the partly blocked waterway, and some ships are switching off their transponders to run the passage.

Why it matters: Hormuz carries the marginal barrel of the oil market. A ceasefire this threadbare keeps a war-risk premium priced into crude, tanker rates and marine insurance for as long as the strikes continue.

Economy

Brent jumps back above $76 as the strikes reverse oil's retreat2

Brent's September contract rose more than 3% to $76.48 a barrel, its highest since June 23 and its first close above $76 in two weeks. One analyst quoted expects traffic through the strait to stay below half of pre-war levels for months, with periodic flare-ups.

Why it matters: This is the war premium made explicit. A sustained halving of Hormuz throughput reprices energy and feeds straight back into the goods-and-services inflation the Fed has yet to tame.

World

Ukraine burns Russian tankers in the Sea of Azov3

Ukraine's General Staff said overnight drones hit 12 Russian tankers, a tug and a cargo ship in the Azov, vessels it says supplied fuel to Russian forces; Moscow acknowledged two tankers damaged. Zelensky confirmed parallel strikes on depots in Stavropol and Tver, a pumping station in Bashkortostan and a loading terminal in Rostov.

Why it matters: Kyiv has moved the war from the front line to Russia's fuel logistics. Squeezing refining and export capacity is a second lever on the same oil market Hormuz is straining.

Washington

Trump asks the Supreme Court to rehear birthright citizenship4

On Truth Social the president said he will seek a rehearing of Trump v. Barbara, the end-of-term decision that struck down his order restricting citizenship for children born on US soil. The Court has not agreed to rehear an already-argued case since 1965.

Why it matters: A 61-year drought makes the bid almost certain to fail, so its force is signaling. For now the Fourteenth Amendment's guarantee stands, and hospitals, employers and parents can rely on it.

Science & Health

Congo's Ebola toll reaches 600 as the virus jumps provinces5

The Democratic Republic of Congo's health ministry reported 600 deaths among 1,759 confirmed cases, with suspected infections now in Tshopo and Haut-Uele beyond the Ituri epicenter. The strain is Bundibugyo, for which no vaccine or treatment is approved; Africa CDC calls it the continent's fastest-growing outbreak.

Why it matters: A vaccine-less strain spreading to new provinces is the scenario global health planners most fear. It lengthens the emergency and raises the odds of cross-border spread.

Wildcard

OpenAI ships ChatGPT Work and the GPT-5.6 family6

The new agent runs long, multi-step jobs and produces documents, slides, sheets and websites; its top "ultra" mode coordinates four agents in parallel. The flagship GPT-5.6 Sol lists at $5 and $30 per million input and output tokens and, OpenAI says, hits its coding and agent benchmarks using fewer than half the output tokens of rival models.

Why it matters: The pitch is performance-per-dollar and autonomy, not a benchmark crown. For anyone building on these models, the token-efficiency and agent-orchestration claims are the numbers worth testing before believing.
